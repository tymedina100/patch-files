## Objections Section

### Heading
Simple where it should be. Careful where it counts.

### Objection: “Don’t other apps already track prices?”
Yes. Some broad card platforms already include price tracking or alerts. The difference is focus: this is built around a buyer’s watchlist, not an entire portfolio, scanner, or market dashboard.

### Objection: “How do I know the prices are accurate?”
That’s the core product promise. Alerts should be tied to recent sold comps and include enough context for you to judge the signal yourself. We will prioritize transparency over black-box pricing.

### Objection: “I can already check eBay sold listings.”
You can — and many collectors do. This saves the repeated manual checking for cards you already care about.

### Objection: “Will this be too expensive?”
Early access will start simple, with pricing shaped by user feedback. The goal is to offer a lightweight tool that feels worth it for collectors who want to save time and avoid overpaying.

## FAQ

### What is the card price-drop watchlist?
It is a focused watchlist for cards you want to buy. Add a card, set your target price, and get notified when market movement suggests it has dropped into your buy zone.

### Is this for sports cards, Pokémon, or TCG?
The product is being designed for sports cards and major TCG categories, including Pokémon. Early coverage may be limited while we validate the highest-demand categories and data quality.

### How is this different from a portfolio tracker?
Portfolio trackers help you manage cards you already own. This helps you monitor cards you want to buy.

### What prices will alerts be based on?
The goal is to use recent sold comps rather than active listing prices alone. Buyers need realistic market signals, not inflated asking prices.

### Can I track graded cards?
Yes, graded-card tracking is a key use case. The watchlist should support grade-specific targets because a PSA 10, PSA 9, raw copy, and other variations can behave like different markets.

### Will you send alerts for every tiny movement?
No. The product is intended to alert when a card reaches your target range, not spam you with every small fluctuation.

### Do I need to upload my whole collection?
No. This is intentionally lightweight. You can use it just for the cards you’re considering buying.

### Is this available now?
Early access is opening soon. Join the list to help shape the product and get invited when beta spots are available.

### Will it be free?
Joining early access is free. Future pricing will depend on data costs, alert volume, and user feedback, but the product is being designed to stay lightweight and accessible.
