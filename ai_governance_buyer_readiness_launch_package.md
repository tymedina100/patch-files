## 10. One-page do-not-say list

### Do not make legal-advice claims

Avoid:
- "This is legal advice."
- "This will satisfy your legal obligations."
- "Use this instead of counsel."
- "This covers all AI laws and regulations."

Use instead:
- "This is a practical readiness resource."
- "It can help teams prepare for internal conversations and buyer diligence."
- "For legal interpretation, consult qualified counsel."

### Do not claim compliance, certification, or audit readiness

Avoid:
- "Become compliant."
- "Guarantees compliance."
- "Audit-ready AI governance kit."
- "Certified AI governance package."
- "Pass any audit."
- "Complete compliance solution."

Use instead:
- "Prepare for AI governance questions."
- "Organize your readiness narrative and evidence."
- "Support internal preparation for buyer diligence and vendor review."

### Do not guarantee buyer approval or sales outcomes

Avoid:
- "Get approved by enterprise buyers."
- "Guarantees procurement approval."
- "Close more deals automatically."
- "Remove all buyer objections."
- "Pass every vendor review."

Use instead:
- "Prepare for buyer diligence conversations."
- "Help your team respond more clearly to governance questions."
- "Reduce last-minute scrambling when buyer requests arrive."

### Do not overstate completeness

Avoid:
- "Everything you need for AI governance."
- "Full implementation kit."
- "Complete AI governance program."
- "All policies, controls, and procedures included."

Use instead:
- "Report and template set."
- "Practical starting point for readiness."
- "Designed to help teams organize, assess, and prepare."

### Do not imply official endorsement

Avoid:
- "Approved by regulators."
- "Government-recognized framework."
- "Meets all 2026 AI requirements."
- "Official compliance standard."

Use instead:
- "Built for teams preparing for 2026 compliance pressure."
- "Designed around practical buyer diligence and vendor review needs."

### Do not use fear-based or absolute claims

Avoid:
- "Buy this or lose enterprise deals."
- "Every company will be required to have this."
- "You are exposed without it."
- "This eliminates AI risk."

Use instead:
- "AI governance questions are becoming more common in buyer diligence."
- "This helps teams prepare earlier and communicate more clearly."
- "Useful for teams that want a practical readiness resource before the next vendor review."

### Safe positioning language

Use:
- "Practical AI governance readiness report and template set."
- "Built for B2B teams preparing for buyer diligence and vendor review."
- "Helpful for SaaS, HR tech, fintech, health tech, AI tooling, enterprise AI, GRC/compliance, and vendor-risk teams."
- "Supports preparation for 2026 compliance pressure."
- "Not legal advice, not an audit, not certification, and not a guarantee of compliance or buyer approval."
